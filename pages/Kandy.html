Kandy

Overview

Kandy, a UNESCO World Heritage site in Sri Lanka, is renowned for its cultural and historical significance. The city's centerpiece is the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, housing a revered relic of Buddha. Surrounded by lush hills, Kandy offers stunning scenery, including Kandy Lake and the Peradeniya Botanical Gardens. The annual Esala Perahera is a vibrant festival featuring traditional dancers and decorated elephants. Nature enthusiasts can explore the Udawattakele Forest Reserve, while tea lovers can visit the Ceylon Tea Museum. Kandy is a perfect blend of culture, history, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination in Sri Lanka.

Attractions areas in Kandy

Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa)

The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa) in Kandy is a major Buddhist site, housing a revered relic believed to be Buddha’s tooth. Built in the late 16th century, the temple features traditional Sinhalese architecture and is part of the royal palace complex. It is renowned for daily rituals and the grand Esala Perahera festival. As a UNESCO World Heritage site, it holds immense historical and cultural importance, attracting pilgrims and tourists alike.

Peradeniya Botanical Gardens

The Peradeniya Botanical Gardens, located just outside Kandy, is one of Sri Lanka’s most beautiful and expansive gardens. Established in 1821, the gardens span over 60 hectares and feature a diverse collection of plants, including orchids, palm trees, and medicinal plants. Highlights include the Avenue of Royal Palms, the Giant Bamboo, and the Orchid House. The gardens are also known for their lush landscapes, tranquil lakes, and historical significance. It’s a popular spot for leisurely walks, picnics, and enjoying Sri Lanka’s rich biodiversity.

Ambuluwawa Tower

The Ambuluwawa Tower is a unique and striking structure located atop Ambuluwawa Mountain 20 kilometers from Kandy. Standing at 48 meters tall, the tower offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including mountains, valleys, and distant towns. It is part of the Ambuluwawa Biodiversity Complex, which encompasses various religious sites including a Buddhist stupa, a Hindu kovil, a Christian church, and a Muslim mosque, symbolizing interfaith harmony. The tower and its surroundings are popular for hiking and exploring Sri Lanka’s natural beauty.
