HortantPlains

Overview

Horton Plains is a stunning national park located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. It's known for its beautiful landscapes, diverse wildlife, and cool climate.The circular trail around the park is about 9 km long and takes around 3-4 hours to complete. It's a popular trek that takes you through diverse landscapes, including grasslands, forests, and along the edges of cliffs.Horton Plains is a paradise for birdwatchers, with many endemic species found in the park.Horton Plains is part of the Central Highlands of Sri Lanka, which was declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2010 due to its biodiversity and ecological significance.

Climate

The climate at Horton Plains in Sri Lanka is cool and misty, largely due to its high elevation, which ranges between 2,100 to 2,300 meters (about 6,900 to 7,500 feet) above sea level. Here's a breakdown of the climate conditions:

Daytime:

Temperatures during the day typically range between 10°C to 20°C (50°F to 68°F).

Nighttime:

At night, temperatures can drop to around 5°C (41°F) or even lower, especially during the winter months (December to February).

How to get HortantPlains

By Train:

A train from Colombo Fort Railway Station to Nanu Oya Railway Station, which is near Nuwara Eliya. The train ride is scenic, passing through tea plantations and mountain views. From Nanu Oya, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Nuwara Eliya. From Nuwara Eliya, it's best to hire a taxi or arrange for a private vehicle to take you to Horton Plains, which is about a 1-1.5 hour drive.

By Bus:

Take a bus from Colombo to Nuwara Eliya. The journey takes around 6-7 hours. From Nuwara Eliya, you can hire a taxi or take a local bus to Horton Plains.

Attractions areas in HortantPlains

World's End

World's End is one of the most iconic and breathtaking viewpoints located within Horton Plains National Park in Sri Lanka. It’s a sheer cliff with a drop of about 870 meters (2,854 feet) that offers spectacular panoramic views of the surrounding landscape.

Baker's Falls

Baker's Falls is a beautiful waterfall located within Horton Plains National Park in Sri Lanka. Named after Sir Samuel Baker, a British explorer and big game hunter who was instrumental in the development of Nuwara Eliya, Baker's Falls is one of the key attractions in the park.
